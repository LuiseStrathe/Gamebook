{# How To #}

How to use GamesBook

1
Register a Group

Register a new group account to start your own GamesBook, if you don't already have one. This will take about 2 minutes, you won't even need an email address.

A group of up to 10 players can share this account right away. You have to start with at least 2 players but can add more or change them anytime.

Register here

2
Play & Log

Time to game!

First, choose a game you want to play and game it.
Then, make sure to log the results of your game in GamesBook. Logs are a record or note of the game and its results.

Depending on the actual game you play, the information you log (or save) and when you log them will be different. That is why GamesBook offers game modes (more info below).

Here are two examples:

> If you do a puzzle, you will simply puzzle it and measure the time it takes you to finish it. Afterwards, you log your time in GamesBook.

> Say you play Scrabble. This is a round-based, multiplayer game. You can have GamesBook open while you play and enter the points after each round or after a playthrough - you decide. Once you are done, you save the log of the Scrabble session. A good GamesBook game mode for this is rounds.

Try without account

3
Remember & Analyze

Games are over, time to check out what and how you played so far!

GamesBook offers analytics for your group. All players can login and revisit the groups' logged games and results. You can see how the players have performed, developed their gameplay or simply remember the fun you've had playing together.

BTW - GamesBook is a Data Science project, so this will be a core feature to check out.
